Modeling the Risk of Hypertension and Stroke Events with a Birespon Logistic Regression Approach (Case Study at Universitas Airlangga Hospital)

Background of the Problem

Based on BPS data collected from 2017-2022, it was found that the number of deaths due to NCDs in Indonesia was 7.04 million cases (BPS, 2023). One of the most common NCDs suffered by the Indonesian population is hypertension and stroke. According to the 2018 Riskesdas data, the recorded prevalence of hypertension in Indonesia based on the results of measurements in the population aged> 18 years reached 34.1%. This percentage increased from the previous prevalence of hypertension in 2013 which only reached 25.8%. Meanwhile, the prevalence of stroke in Indonesia in 2013 reached 7%, and increased to 10.9% in 2018. Hypertension and stroke are considered as “comorbidities” or diseases that can occur simultaneously in one individual. Hypertension is a major risk factor that can lead to stroke. High systolic and diastolic blood pressure is known to increase the risk of stroke.

Purpose of the Problem

This study aims to describe the risk of hypertension and stroke in RSUA patients and the factors that are thought to influence. The study will model the risk using a birresponse logistic regression approach based on the factors that are thought to be influential. In addition, the study will also analyze and interpret the results of birresponse logistic regression modeling for the incidence of hypertension and stroke.

Benefits of the Research

  • For the health department : As an input in evaluating the current program
  • For other researchers : As literature that can add information and consideration for other researchers in the future that is more specific
  • For the Public : Providing information about the relationship between hypertension and stroke with the factors that influence it factors that influence it so that people are more sensitive to health

Project Results

Conclusion

  • Based on a study of 96 outpatient patients in the cardiology and neurology departments at RSUA, it was found that 60,42% of patients have hypertension and 37,50% have experienced a stroke. The majority of these patients are female, aged 60-74 years, have a BMI of less than 25 kg/m², are categorized as having normal stress levels, are physically active, and do not have a family history of hypertension or stroke
  • The analysis indicates a dependency between the two response variables. Predictor variables such as gender, obesity, stress levels, and family history of hypertension significantly affect the incidence of hypertension and stroke at RSUA. Additionally, the goodness-of-fit test for the bivariate logistic regression model confirms that the model is appropriate
  • Based on the results of the bivariate logistic regression analysis on the in-sample data, an accuracy rate of 74,03% was achieved. Estimating the probability on out-of-sample data yielded an accuracy rate of 84,21%. Overall, the accuracy across all samples was 76,04%

Recommendations

  • For the health department : Can improve early detection efforts by providing regular screening programs for high-risk communities as identified in the model and also strengthen education on healthy lifestyles and the importance of regular controls to individuals
  • For other researchers : Continue the study with a focus on more specific risk factors, such as diet, smoking habits, alcohol consumption, which may not have been included in this model and increase the sample size
  • For the Public : Regular health check-ups are recommended, especially for those with a family history of hypertension. Individuals with high stress should manage it through relaxation techniques, meditation, or counseling. Those with obesity should maintain a healthy weight through a balanced diet and regular exercise
