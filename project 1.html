Understanding of Churn

Customer Churn, or often referred to as churn, refers to the phenomenon where customers or users of a product or service stop using that product or service within a certain period of time. In a business context, “churn” can be defined as losing customers or clients.

Business Understanding

In a business era full of fierce competition and ever-changing markets, understanding and managing customer churn has become one of the critical aspects of marketing strategy and business development. Customer churn not only has a direct impact on revenue, but also damages brand image and affects long-term business sustainability. Customer churn analysis provides an in-depth understanding of customer behavior, their decision patterns, and early signs that indicate possible churn. With this information, companies can take proactive measures to mitigate churn, retain existing customers, and improve overall customer satisfaction.

Purpose of Churn Analysis

  • Identifying patterns of customer behavior related to with churn
  • Improving customer service and experience to reduce the churn rate
  • Develop effective retention strategies based on analysis findings

Conclusion

Based on the features that have been used, feature importance is obtained, namely the use of promos, item prices, customer activities, customer age, purchase time patterns, shipping costs, and payment status. The best model obtained is Decision Tree with Hyperparameter Tuning Grid Search CV. Best Hyperparameter Tuning obtained is {'criterion': 'entropy', 'max_depth': 10, 'min_samples_leaf': 1,'min_samples_split': 2, 'splitter': 'random'}. And obtained the recall evaluation metrics for 0 = 0.88 and 1 = 0.86.

Recommendations

  • Engage customers in promotional programs, including special offers or discounts
  • Evaluate discount policies or product bundling and understand customer sensitivity to changes in price
  • For “active” customers, provide special offers or promos when they are actively shopping. As for “passive I” and “passive II” customers, increase engagement by offering incentive programs or promotions to encourage more transactions and purchase activity
  • Marketing strategies tailored to the age range of customers can increase campaign relevance and customer satisfaction
  • Understand customer buying time patterns and customize marketing strategies or special offers to match their time preferences
  • Evaluation of shipping fee policies, including fee-free or fee-discount options for certain purchases fee or fee discounts for certain purchases
  • Although low impact, further understanding of patterns payment patterns can help in customization of payment strategies, including optimization of payment methods or special offers for on-time payments
